Field Notes Nº 01 — For Physios

You wrote a great plan.
They lost it by Tuesday.

Rehab only works when it gets done. REPCO is a small, honest app that puts your prescription in your patient's pocket — the reps, the form, and a place to log it. No portal. No subscription. No fluff.

01The Gap

It evaporates between your room and theirs.

The handout gets buried. The form gets forgotten. The reps get guessed. You find out it wasn't done at the next appointment — if it comes up at all. Adherence is the quiet variable wrecking your outcomes, and right now you can't see it.
This isn't a motivation problem. It's a tooling problem.
02What it is

A daily checklist they'll actually open.

Built for runners carrying rehab, strength and mileage all at once — the people most likely to skip the boring, important work.
  • 01Every move, fully loaded.Sets, reps and weight on each exercise — with a reference picture and a video so the form survives the car park.
  • 02They tick it off. You get a signal.Patients mark work done and leave a note on how it felt. Adherence stops being invisible.
  • 03Pocket-sized, private, offline.Installs to the home screen, works without signal, and keeps the data on their device — not ours.
  • 04Write it in plain language.Type the week the way you'd say it; it gets structured into a clean, shareable plan.
  • 05Re-issue without erasing.Progress the plan whenever you like — their logged history stays intact.
03What it isn't

We'd rather tell you now.

No vapourware, no roadmap theatre. Here's exactly where it stops today.
  • —No accounts, no cloud.Sharing a plan is a file, not a live link or a chat thread.
  • —The pictures are a guide, not gospel.Reference images come from an open library. Not clinically validated — your judgement still rules.
  • —No pain scale, RPE or asymmetry tracking.Yet. It logs done / not-done and a free-text note.
  • —No dashboards or load monitoring.No graphs, no risk scores, no analytics.
  • —It is not a medical device.And it won't pretend to be one.
04The ask

I don't want applause. I want the holes.

You've seen a hundred apps promise to fix adherence. Pressure-test this one like you'd want to.
  • Q1Can you express a real prescription here — tempo, holds, progressions, the cues that matter?
  • Q2Are the pictures and videos right — or is any of it misleading enough to retrain a bad pattern?
  • Q3Would you hand this to a patient over your current sheet? Where does it fall down?
  • Q4What's the one missing thing between "neat" and "I'd use this on Monday"?
